HiDALGO project was established in order to address this solid demand in the society to know more about global challenges and how to deal with them efficiently. We are an EU funded project which brings together GSS and HPC experts from 13 partner institutions out of 7 countries. The work is coordinated by ATOS Spain and we are in the 3rd year of the project.

HiDALGO Motivation and Main Objectives

Goal: evidence based policy-making for current and upcoming situations via accurate GC simulations

High Level Ambition & Project Targets

  • Benefit from the synergies between HPC, HPDA, AI, and GCs
    • baseline for HPC, HPDA, and AI in the domain of GCs
    • focus on highly accurate models
  • Provide a single entry point for decision makers (and other entities)
    • a multi-domain portal for the GC community
  • Connect & train the different communities

Essentially the goal of the project is to target computer-aided evidence based policy-making for current and upcoming situations via accurate simulation of GCs. The project focuses on 3 main objectives…

Top news of the last months (dominate in media):

Some thinkers tend to reason that famine, plagues, and wars are essentially problems of the former centuries and humanity must look on the challenges of the like future happiness, immortality or God-like powers. In HiDALGO we have a more conservative view. If we take a look on the news that dominate in the media over the last months, we will see pandemics and wars, besides other topics. So these constitute use cases for our research.

Refugee and human migration simulation

  • develop realistic models for simulating refugee streams
  • complete data collection on refugee movements
  • investigate the consequences of a nation closing its borders
  • HPC in migration use case:
    • expensive simulations
    • ensemble runs
  • HPDA in migration use case:
    • pre-process GIS data
    • analyse weather/climate data
    • post-process results
    • synthetic data
  • Usage: CAR, S.Sudan, Mali, Ethiopia

HPC and HPDA is used for different purposes in this use case. In particular, we use HPDA to pre-process geo-spacial information. Later on today, we will have a talk of Christoph Schwimmer this this topic.

COVID and Flu spread simulation

  • simulate epidemics spread across the local area
  • determine people infected, ICU occupation, etc.
  • investigate the effects of applying certain policies (i.e., curfews)

  • HPC in epidemiology use case:
    • expensive simulations
    • ensemble runs
  • HPDA in epidemiology use case:
    • pre-process GIS inputs
    • post-process results
    • analyse weather/climate data
    • synthetic inputs
  • Usage: London, Madrid

There's a lot of hype around infection spread simulation these days and it is one our use cases. Derek Groen will present synopsis of our results in this direction right after my talk.

Urban air pollution simulation

  • simulate pollution in cities based on real-world sensor data
  • couple models for traffic, air flow, and weather simulation
  • design evidence-based decision models to leverage green growth

  • HPC in UAP use case:
    • expensive simulations
    • ensemble runs

  • HPDA in UAP use case:

    • impute the missing data
    • reduce models
    • post-process results
  • Usage: Györ, Graz, Milwaukee

Social network analysis

  • analyze the structure of social networks
  • analyze the stochastic behavior of the message spreading
  • simulate the spread of messages among users
  • study the spread of 'Fake News' and develop countermeasures

  • HPC in SNA use case:
    • expensive network analytics
    • ensemble runs
  • HPDA in SNA use case:
    • features extraction
    • retweet probabilities
    • post-process results
  • Usage: COVID-19 Tweets

HiDALGO Approach and Generalized Workflow

Accurate digital twinning of GCs ⥂ coupled simulations + HPDA

  • models for diverse social and physical phenomena (often multiscale)
  • massive static and streaming data sets

HiDALGO approaches policy-making via accurate digital twinning of GCs. Accurate digital twins for GCs inevitably involve models for diverse social and physical phenomena and consume massive sets of static and streaming data. The generalized HiDALGO workflow is demonstrated on this slide. Such simulation-centric approach gives rise to a number of research topics related to coupling of models and data sources – including coupling across data centers – and to design of HPDA methods.

HiDALGO Tools

In order to implement this approach, we use, develop, and promote a bunch of software tools. You can see some on them listed on this slide. In the domain of simulation we use ABMS and CFD frameworks, as well as specific linear algebra packages, some of which are developed by our researchers. In the domain of DA, we primarily rely on the well established frameworks like Dask and Spark. For visualization, we use our developments – Covise and Visualizer – capable of tackling massive datasets and providing immersive visualization features. Finally, for orchestrating complicated coupled simulation workflows, we use tools like Cloudify and FabSim.
